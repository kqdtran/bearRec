Sex and Gender in Premodern Chinese Culture -- Chinese (CHINESE)181[4units]
Description: This course explores Chinese cultures of sex and gender from antiquity to the eighteenth century. We will look at how sex and gender are treated in the political, moral, medical, and religious discources. Through a variety of literary genres, we will see the complexity of gender differences, the fluidity of desire, and ultimatly the plasticity of the human body. These texts reveal how Chinese writers imaginatively exmined and reinvented what it meant to be men and women.
