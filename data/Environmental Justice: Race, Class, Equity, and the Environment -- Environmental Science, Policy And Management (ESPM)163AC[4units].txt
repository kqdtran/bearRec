Environmental Justice: Race, Class, Equity, and the Environment -- Environmental Science, Policy And Management (ESPM)163AC[4units]
Description: Overview of the field of environmental justice, analyzing the implications of race, class, labor, and equity on environmental degradation and regulation. Environmental justice movements and struggles within poor and people of color communities in the U.S., including: African Americans, Latino Americans, and Native American Indians. Frameworks and methods for analyzing race, class, and labor. Cases of environmental injustice, community and government responses, and future strategies for achieving environmental and labor justice. Also listed as Sociology 137AC.
