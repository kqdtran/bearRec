Behavioral Ecology -- Integrative Biology (INTEGBI)146[3units]
Description: An in-depth examination of the ecological and evolutionary bases for behavioral diversity. Topics covered include behavior as an adaptive response, sexual selection, animal mating system, group living, and cooperative and competitive interactions. Current conceptual approaches to these topics are explored, with an emphasis upon rigorous testing of hypotheses drawn from the primary literature. Discussion sections are used to explore selected topics in greater detail and to stimulate critical review of recent research in behavioral ecology. Two midterms plus several discussion-based written assignments.
