Art and Identity in the United States -- History Of Art (HISTART)187AC[3units]
Description: The course examines a series of artistic formations and critiques of identity among the diverse collection of individuals, cultures, and institutions that composed the United States from the Colonial Era to the Cold War.  Beginning with the hybrid material culture of Mission Dolores in eighteenth-century New Spain, the course progress chronologically and thematically through a sequence of case studies.  Material, formal, social, and political contexts will all be considered as we address a wide range of art practices and media, including, among others, sculpture, land art, costume, mural painting, photography, architecture, and conceptual art.
